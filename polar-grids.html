Bobbin lace and other grid examples

The examples with B-numbers match "Viele Gute Gründe" by Ulrike Löhr. She specifies the angles relative to the top of a patch of lace, while this application applies the angle to the footside of the doily edging. So we have to subtract Ulrike's angles from 90 degrees.

  • default
  • Torchon, Flanders: B1 any angle between about 15-75 degrees is possible
  • Small snowflakes: B2 other angles result in other width/height ratios of the rectangles
  • Binche/Duchesse: B3, B4-B6, B7
  • Rotated hexagons: with triangles, with circles with triangles and circles

Help information

A web form to assemble the uri-query is not yet implemented. So you have to assemble the tail of the address manually in your browser. None of the parameters is mandatory and the order of the parameters is not relevant. A default is applied for missing or invalid values. You can start with one of the linked examples, and subsequently add/change the parameters. The #B.. parts of the addresses are not significant, they serve as a reminder but must be last.

Parameters innerDiameter, outerDiameter

The unit for the diameters is mm when printed at 100%. The size on screen might vary from monitor to monitor. The actual inner value might be larger.

Parameter Angle

The unit for angle is degrees.

Parameter dotsPerRing

A dotPattern may introduce repeat factors. The desired pattern of lace may also introduce repeat factors. The number of dots should be a multitude of the repeat factor(s) to prevent irregularities where the end of the grid or piece of lace meets the the start. The start of the grid is the horizontal axis on the right side.

Parameter dotPattern

The dotPattern defines whether and how dots are plotted. Each sequence of digits applies to one ring of dots. A one means plot as a normal dot, a two means plot as a circle, any other digit (or character) means skip the dot. Each sequence of digits introduces a repeat factor equal to the length of the sequence.
